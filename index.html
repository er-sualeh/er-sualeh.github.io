// About

From dissertation to deployed fleets.

I'm a robotics engineer who has spent the last decade moving between research labs and production floors — from autonomous driving perception at Chungbuk National University to commercial service robots now running in the field for RGT Inc.

My PhD centered on semantics-aware dynamic SLAM, fusing 3D LiDAR and vision to track moving objects in cluttered, real-world scenes. That research became the foundation for how I now build navigation stacks: systems that don't just plan a path, but understand the space they're moving through.

Today I lead ROS2/Nav2 navigation development at RGT, architecting everything from sensor filtering and localization to fleet-level multi-robot coordination — shipped and running across multiple countries.
